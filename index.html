Strengths

Weaknesses

  • Good proximity to city centre and other up and coming areas

  • The site has good access to the bus network

  • 25% of housing units are socially rented

  • Energy efficiency of housing on site is high (especially towards Ancoats/New Islington)

  • Ultra high speed internet availability and high number of e-professionals

  • Cheaper average house prices than the immediate surroundings

  • Vast historic and heritage value from industrialisation periods

  • Existing good quality green infrastructure, accessible by pedestrians 

  • Overall, good access to hospitals, doctors surgeries, primary schools and religious institutions 

  • The Sportcity is regarded as important by the council and the neighbourhood has been designated as a major ‘Recreational Route’

  • The ward is the most deprived of all wards in Manchester LA and ranks in the top 1% of deprived areas in the IMD

  • Poor GCSE achievement, high unemployment rate, and many work in low skilled occupations 

  • Domestic and non-domestic energy consumption has decreased onsite at a slower rate than Manchester’s average

  • Fuel poverty increases towards Newton Heath

  • Lack of green network connecting the green infrastructure 

  • Lack of easy access to existing farmlands

  • The site is mostly residential with little mixed-use development

  • Most houses heated by gas

  • Few electric vehicle charging points 

  • Lack of amateur sporting facilities, one public library with limited resources

  • The site lacks tram network coverage

  • At the edge of the site, there is a demand for more GPs, dentists and pharmacies

Opportunities

Threats/Challenges

  • Brownfield land and unused buildings are opportunities for development. 

  • The area is favourable for mixed-use development

  • The Co-op Live Arena is currently being constructed on site and will host an innovative heat network

  • Existing high quality green infrastructure could be enhanced by creating green networks and combining urban farms, and the Medlock river valley is a “green infrastructure opportunity area”

  • An opportunity to help the city achieve its net zero goals by focussing on sustainable transport networks

  • A number of existing proposals to improve public transport, green infrastructure, and leisure facilities in the area, which may improve attractiveness of the site

  • Scope to increase educational attainment and new business opportunities

  • Educational attainment and proficiency in English are a barrier to  quality employment

  • New developments aimed at affluent buyers could result in gentrification and threaten existing social cohesion and sense of community 

  • If the rate of energy consumption continues to reduce more slowly than the rest of the city, this may be problematic for Manchester’s zero carbon goals

  • Flooding is a challenge in the Medlock valley and the Irk valley

  • Fear of unsafe environment as a barrier to access green spaces 

  • Affluent residents prefer cars as their main mode of transport which may threaten sustainable transport goals

  • There are only 2 police stations located relatively far away from the site - this could increase response time and increase crime rates
